Marquam Heritage Trail

The northern section of this Portland City park borders on the Oregon Health Sciences University Campus off of Sam Jackson Parkway and Terwilliger Boulevard and encompasses two ravines on the slopes of Marquam Hill. The park is named after Philip Marquam, who was a Multnomah County judge in the 1860s and later served in the Oregon State Legislature. Longer hikes are possible as the Marquam Trail runs through here and up to Council Crest, eventually connecting to the Wildwood Trail just north of the Oregon Zoo. The Marquam Trail is part of Portland's 40-mile Loop.
