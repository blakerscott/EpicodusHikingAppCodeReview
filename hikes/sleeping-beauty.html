Sleeping Beauty Trail

From the Trout Lake Valley, the profile of Sleeping Beauty resembles a maiden in repose, her petrified locks cascading down the south slope, her tears no doubt the bubbling source of Skull Creek. The short, steep hike up here, mostly switchbacking in shady woods until you get to the last tightly coiled section of the ascent, is a good family outing and rewards with expansive views of the Cascades, the spine of Indian Heaven, and the settled valleys to the south.

Plan Your Trip

    Trail Aspects:
  • Exertion: Difficult, 3 Miles Round-Trip
  • Sights: Beautiful views of Mt. Adams and the forests of the Cascades
  • Popularity: Moderate
  • Open: All Year
    Directions From Portland:
  • Travel Time: approximately 110 minutes (100 Miles from Portland)
  • Get on I-84 E
  • Take exit 64
  • Cross the Hood River Bridge into Washington
  • Take WA 141 N, Trout Lake Creek Rd. amd NF-8810 to NF-040
  • Continue on this road until you see signs for the trailhead
