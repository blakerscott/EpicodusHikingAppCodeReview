Trout Lake Ice Cave

At over 6,500 feet, Lookout Mountain is much higher than most of the smaller peaks that surround Mount Hood. In fact, it is the second highest peak in the Mt. Hood National Forest. The added elevation brings broad meadows, expansive views, alpine wildflowers and ancient whitebark pines to the scene. The view of Mount Hood, just seven miles to the west, is from a unique perspective that reveals the massive bulk of the mountain

Plan Your Trip

    Trail Aspects:
  • Exertion: Difficult, 0.25 Miles Round-Trip
  • Sights: Ice stalagtites and stalagmites, cave creatures
  • Popularity: Low
  • Open: May - September
    Fees:
  • $5 NW Pass (Available at the trailhead)
    Ammenities:
  • Bathrooms
  • Picnic Area
    Prepare! The caves are FREEZING even in the summer; make sure to bring:
  • Coats
  • Headlights/Flashlights
  • Good climbing shoes with traction (these are ICE caves)
    Directions From Portland:
  • Travel Time: Approximately 100 minutes from downtown (110 miles from Portland)
  • Get on I-84 E to Hood River
  • Cross the Hood River Bridge to the Washington side
  • Go left onto Highway 14, and follow it onto Highway Alt 14
  • Drive north for 21 miles to Trout Lake
  • Stay left at the gas station, driving another 6.5 miles
  • Keep straight when 141 becomes FS 24. Look for the Ice Cave sign and turn left into the parkingl lot
